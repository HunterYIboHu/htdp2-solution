Exercise 117. Explain why the following sentences are syntactically legal expressions
1.x
Explain: x represent the variable, is not one of the keywords.

2.(= y z)
Explain: '=' is a pre-defined function, can be the first parameter;
and y, z means the function parameter can be the variable.

3.(= (= y z) 0)
Explain: like the one above, but the parameter of outside function '=' is the result of expression '(= y z)'
